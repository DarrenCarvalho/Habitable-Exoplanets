About

Habitable Exoplanets are the planets which have similar characteristics to Earth in terms of chemical composition and are at an optimum distance form the parent star for liquid water to exist. The following algorithm is to predict the potential candidates for Habitable Exoplanets.

To predict whether a planet is habitable or not, you can refer to the Dataset Examples link in the reference.

OR

Copy the row values from the table below.

In the Column 'habitable' (Acutal Values) :

  • 1 : The planet is habitable
  • 0 : The planet is not habitable

Compare the output in the form window with the habitable column to check if the prediction was right.

If input is given from a different data source take these units into consideration for correct outputs:

  • Planet Orbital Period (In Earth Years)
  • Planet Radius ( In terms of Jupiter)
  • Distance to Star ( pc (1pc = 3.26156ly))
  • Temperature of Star (K)
  • Radius of Star ( In terms of Solar Radii )
  • Luminosity of Star ( Log(Solar) )
